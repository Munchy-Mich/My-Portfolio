FlatShare: Affordable Shared Housing Platform

Connecting Young Professionals with Affordable Shared Housing in Abuja

Role: Product Manager/Owner
Team: Individual project
Focus: Digital marketplace for shared accommodations

Project Overview

FlatShare is a digital marketplace platform designed to connect property owners with young professionals seeking affordable shared accommodations in Abuja, Nigeria. As a solo product management project, I developed this initiative from conception through MVP planning, addressing a critical housing accessibility gap in Nigeria's capital city.

Target Market: Young professionals earning ₦150,000-₦350,000 monthly

Projected Impact: 40% cost reduction compared to traditional rentals

Timeline: Beta testing September 2025, Launch October 2025

Investment Target: ₦75 million Series A funding

Problem Identification & Definition

The Challenge

Young professionals in Abuja face a severe housing crisis where average rental costs consume 60-70% of their monthly income. This forces them to choose between unaffordable formal housing options and unsafe informal arrangements, with minimal digital solutions available to bridge this gap.

Key Problem Indicators:

  • Housing supply meets less than 20% of market demand
  • Traditional rental agencies charge 10% of annual rent in fees
  • Limited digital platforms for verified shared accommodations

Research & Discovery

Research Methodology

I conducted comprehensive market research to validate the problem space and identify user needs:

  • 47 structured interviews with young professionals in Abuja
  • 1,200+ rental listing analysis across existing platforms
  • 35 property owner surveys on rental preferences
  • Competitive landscape mapping of Nigerian housing solutions

Key Research Findings

  • 78% of young professionals spend over 50% of income on housing
  • 92% would consider shared accommodations if properly vetted
  • Property owners could increase yields by 35-40% through room sharing
  • Existing platforms lack robust verification and matching capabilities
