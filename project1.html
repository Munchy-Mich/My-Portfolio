The Pivot Nigeria Websites Development

Role: Product Manager
Duration: 1 week (6 days)
Team Size: 15 members

Project Overview

Led the development of two responsive landing pages for The Pivot Nigeria, a civic-inspired movement focused on African transformation. The project involved creating the main organizational website and a dedicated website for their flagship MSME digital transformation program.

Timeline: Delivered on schedule

Quality: Zero post-launch critical bugs

Scope: 100% of requirements met

Target: 3,000 MSMEs for The Pivot Challenge program

Problem Statement

The Pivot Nigeria needed a digital presence to support their mission of nurturing changemakers across Africa. They required:

  • A main website to showcase their movement and initiatives
  • A dedicated platform for The Pivot Challenge program targeting 3,000 MSMEs
  • Mobile-optimized, conversion-focused landing pages
  • Integration capabilities for forms, newsletters, and social media

Team Structure

Cross-functional team composition:

  • Product Designers (4)
  • Frontend Developers (3)
  • Backend Developers (4)
  • Quality Assurance Analyst (1)
  • Data Analyst (1)
  • Digital Marketer (1)
  • Stakeholder: The Pivot Organisation leadership team

Project Scope

Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Tight Timeline

Solution: Prioritized Challenge website development first as it had immediate application deadlines, followed by main website completion.

Challenge 2: Multiple Stakeholder Requirements

Solution: Established clear communication protocols and daily check-ins to ensure alignment across all parties.

Challenge 3: Content Integration

Solution: Developed flexible content templates that allowed for easy updates without developer intervention.

Results and Impact

Delivery Metrics

Timeline: Delivered on schedule

Quality: Zero post-launch critical bugs

Scope: 100% of requirements met

Team Satisfaction: All stakeholders approved deliverables

Key Outcomes

  • Successfully launched both websites before deadline
  • Enabled online application process for 3,000 MSME target
  • Established digital foundation for community building
  • Created scalable platform for future program iterations

Lessons Learned

  • Stakeholder Alignment: Regular communication prevented scope creep and ensured clear expectations
  • Agile Delivery: Breaking project into phases allowed for faster iteration and feedback
  • Cross-functional Collaboration: Daily standups kept all team members aligned on progress
  • User-Centric Design: Focus on mobile optimization was crucial for target audience accessibility

Status: Successfully delivered and launched
