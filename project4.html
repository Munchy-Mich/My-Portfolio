ShantyBay

Empowering Nigeria's Small Retailers for the Digital Economy

Timeline: 7 days (May 2025)
Role: Product Manager/Owner

Overview

63% increase in perceived vendor trustworthiness

2.5x higher purchase intent among test users

$13B market opportunity in Nigeria's e-commerce sector

Problem

Small retailers in Nigeria's urban and peri-urban areas lack accessible digital commerce solutions that integrate inventory management, payments, and delivery logistics, preventing them from participating in the growing digital economy.

Solution

A mobile-first marketplace platform that enables traditional retailers to create digital storefronts, manage inventory, process payments, and coordinate deliveries through an intuitive, trust-centric interface.

Market Context & Selection

I selected Nigeria's retail market as an unfamiliar but high-potential opportunity. Nigeria represents Africa's largest economy with over 200 million people, where 80% of retail transactions still occur through informal markets despite rapid digital infrastructure growth.

Key Market Indicators

  • E-commerce market: $13B (2023), growing at 14.5% CAGR
  • Internet penetration: 110M users (50%+ population)
  • Mobile payment growth: 78% YoY increase (2022-2023)
  • Smartphone adoption: 40% and climbing

The market selection revealed a critical gap: while digital solutions exist for formal urban merchants, independent local retailers remain underserved despite representing the economy's backbone.

Problem Discovery & Research

Research Methodology

I conducted user research using a human-centered design approach over 2 days:

Primary Research:

  • 6 semi-structured stakeholder interviews (10-30 minutes each)
  • Contextual inquiry observing current workflows
  • Cross-segment validation across urban and semi-urban areas

Participant Breakdown:

  • 4 Small business owners (ages 30-55, mixed gender)
  • 1 Regular online shopper (age 28, female, Lagos)
  • 1 Delivery driver (age 32, male, urban area)

Key Findings

Research revealed four critical pain points affecting local commerce:

Manual Inventory Management

"I use a notebook for everything. I don't even know what I'm out of until it's too late." - Adebayo, Shop Owner

Impact: Missed sales opportunities, inefficient restocking

Low Social Media ROI

High activity but poor conversion rates and expensive, complex logistics on existing platforms

Payment & Delivery Friction

"Bank transfers waste time and scare customers" - Folake, Vendor

Insecure payment methods and poor delivery coordination

Digital Literacy Barriers

Non-intuitive tools rejected by busy merchants. High workload requires immediate utility for adoption

Problem Statement

Target Users: Independent retailers with 1-5 employees who sell physical goods, have smartphone access, but lack integrated, accessible, and affordable retail tech solutions.

Core Problem: Small retailers cannot participate in Nigeria's growing digital economy due to fragmented tools and high barriers to entry, resulting in 40% annual revenue growth losses and elevated customer stress from uncertain product availability.

Solution Development & Ideation

Solution Exploration

I generated and evaluated 5 solution concepts using an Impact vs. Feasibility matrix:

Top Solutions:

  • ShantyBay - Comprehensive marketplace platform (Score: 31/40)
  • RetailConnect - B2B wholesale connector (Score: 28/40)
  • PayTrack - Payment-focused solution (Score: 28/40)

Decision Rationale: ShantyBay offered the strongest network effects and revenue diversity despite requiring higher initial investment.

Design Principles

Three core principles guided development:

  • Mobile-first, low-barrier design for vendors with limited digital skills
  • Trust-centric architecture with reviews, verification, and direct communication
  • Seamless integration of inventory, payments, and logistics through local partnerships

MVP Development & Testing

Core Feature Set

Based on user feedback, I prioritized these MVP features:

For Vendors:

  • Photo-based storefront setup
  • Inventory alerts and basic analytics
  • Multi-channel payment integration
  • Delivery coordination system

For Customers:

  • Direct messaging with vendors
  • Trust mechanisms (reviews, verification)
  • Real-time inventory visibility
  • Secure checkout process

Prototype Development

I created low-fidelity prototypes using Canva, focusing on the critical Vendor Profile Screen as the primary trust-building touchpoint.

Mockups

User Testing & Iteration

Objective: Validate whether the design addressed core user concern around trust in local vendors.

Testing Method:

  • 3 user testing sessions with target customers
  • Focus on Vendor Profile Screen interactions
  • Pre/post trust perception measurements

Key Insights:

  • Users demanded transparency: "lack of transparency = lack of trust"
  • Need for business credibility signals beyond basic contact info
  • Stock visibility critical for purchase decisions

Design Iterations

Based on testing feedback, I implemented these enhancements:

Pain Point Original Design Post-Iteration Enhancement
Business credibility Just vendor name/logo CAC registration number, years in business, completed orders
Trust signals No reviews/context Recent customer reviews with real names and ratings
Stock clarity No inventory indicator Live inventory status (in-stock/out-of-stock)
Communication Hidden contact links Prominent call and message buttons
Transparency Sparse vendor info Business hours, response time, "About vendor" section

Product Strategy & Roadmap

Success Metrics

Primary KPIs:

  • Monthly Active Vendors (MAV)
  • Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
  • Gross Merchandise Value (GMV)
  • Vendor-to-customer conversion rate

Secondary Metrics:

  • Time to first sale for new vendors
  • Customer retention rate
  • Average order value
  • Platform trust score

6-12 Month Roadmap

Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Foundation & Growth

  • Scale to 5 additional Nigerian cities
  • Reach 10,000+ consumer connections
  • Integrate escrow payment system

Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Enhancement & Retention

  • Launch vendor analytics dashboard
  • Implement review response system
  • Pilot localized marketing campaigns

Phase 3 (Months 7-12): Expansion & Monetization

  • Multilingual support (Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa)
  • Vendor subscription tiers with premium features
  • Offline-first capabilities for unstable internet areas

Key Learnings & Retrospective

Critical Success Factors

  • Trust is Non-Negotiable: Verified vendors saw 3x more consumer interactions than unverified ones
  • Simplicity Drives Adoption: Users preferred clear navigation over complex features
  • Human Touch Required: Automated onboarding insufficient; hybrid approach needed
  • Infrastructure Resilience: Internet instability requires offline-first planning

Challenges & Mitigation

Challenge: Low digital literacy among target vendors

Mitigation: Implemented hybrid onboarding with community ambassadors

Challenge: Infrastructure limitations affecting real-time features

Solution: Prioritized offline-first capabilities and caching mechanisms

Future Considerations

  • Expansion to other West African markets
  • Integration with existing mobile money providers
  • Partnership opportunities with logistics companies
  • Potential for B2B wholesale marketplace features

Results & Impact

Quantitative Results:

  • 63% increase in perceived vendor trustworthiness
  • 2.5x higher purchase intent among test users
  • 100% of testers engaged with messaging features
  • Validated problem-solution fit within 7-day timeline

Qualitative Impact:

  • Clear path to market for underserved vendor segment
  • Framework for trust-building in informal markets
  • Validated mobile-first approach for emerging market commerce

Business Case: ShantyBay addresses a $13B market opportunity with 14.5% annual growth, targeting millions of underserved retailers across Nigeria.
